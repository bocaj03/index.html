The Big Picture

Glazier Ventures operates one of the larger Hello Sugar franchise portfolios, but the P&L tells a tale of two very different businesses under one roof. Two locations — Edgewater and Academy — generate the vast majority of the value, each producing $450k+ in annualized service revenue with healthy margins north of 25%. The remaining six active locations are a mixed bag ranging from acceptable to deeply problematic.

The most urgent concern among the newer locations is Littleton — open 13 months and still running rent and advertising at a combined 34.8% of revenue, which is too high for a location at this stage. Glendale, by contrast, is a brand-new flagship that opened in September 2025 and is only 6 months in — its numbers look alarming on a raw percentage basis but are broadly normal for a ramp-up. Cherry Creek is the most frustrating story: solid revenue but near-zero profit due to the highest commission rate and royalty rate in the portfolio.

The good news: the two flagship locations are genuinely strong, and Aurora, Westminster, and Boulder are all producing positive results with room to improve. This portfolio has a clear path to higher profitability — it runs through fixing or exiting the underperformers.

03Where You're Winning
★
Edgewater
Highest Revenue in the Portfolio — Best Ad Efficiency at $15.10 per Ad Dollar
Edgewater leads the portfolio in both volume ($566k service revenue, ~$486k annualized) and ad efficiency. At $15.10 in revenue generated per $1 of ad spend — vs. $5.90 at Lone Tree and $2.40 at Glendale (which is only 6 months old) — Edgewater's marketing is working exceptionally well. Rent at 6.4% of revenue gives strong operating leverage. The one flag: Edgewater is the only location with a Medical Director fee ($4,100), suggesting GFE/laser services. This revenue stream and associated cost structure should be understood and factored into any operational decisions.
★
Academy
Best Rent Structure in the Portfolio at 2.6% of Revenue — Exceptional Operating Leverage
Academy's rent of $989/month against $524k in service revenue is the most favorable rent-to-revenue ratio in the entire portfolio. Combined with disciplined commission rates (25.7%) and solid ad efficiency ($12.90/ad $), Academy generates the highest estimated NOI margin at ~26%. It is exactly what a mature Hello Sugar location should look like. The primary concern is $13,707 in interest expense — the highest of any location by far — which warrants understanding. Is this a build-out loan? Line of credit? It's reducing bottom-line income that would otherwise push margins even higher.
✓
Aurora · Westminster · Boulder
Three Solid Mid-Tier Locations Running 15–18% Estimated NOI Margins
Aurora ($253k annualized), Westminster ($233k annualized), and Boulder ($165k annualized) — all open for the full 14 months are all running profitably with NOI margins in the 15–18% range. Commission rates are well-controlled (24–27%), and ad spend is reasonable. None of these are spectacular, but all three are doing what a franchise should do: generating consistent cash flow. The opportunity in all three is incremental — slight improvements in ad efficiency or commission management could push each one meaningfully higher.
✓
Portfolio-Wide
Royalty Rates Are Consistent — Cherry Creek Is the Outlier Worth Investigating
Seven of the eight active locations pay royalties at 5.8–6.2% of service revenue, which is consistent and expected. Cherry Creek is the clear outlier at 7.4%. This either reflects a different royalty tier, a penalty structure, or a calculation anomaly worth flagging to Hello Sugar corporate. At Cherry Creek's revenue level, every 1% of excess royalty costs roughly $2,169 annually — meaningful when margins are already thin.
04Problems That Need Immediate Attention
🔴
Littleton
13 Months In and Still Spending 34.8% of Revenue on Rent + Ads Combined
Littleton opened in February 2025 and has been running for 13 months. Over that period it generated $111,068 in service revenue ($8,544/month). The problem isn't the revenue level for a 13-month location — it's the cost structure. Rent runs $1,471/month (17.2% of revenue) and advertising costs $1,499/month (17.5% of revenue), meaning rent and ads alone consume 34.8% of every dollar before commissions, supplies, royalties, or any other expense. At 13 months, a location should be seeing these ratios improve as revenue grows into the fixed cost base. If Littleton's monthly revenue isn't trending meaningfully upward, a structural reassessment is warranted. Set a clear 6-month revenue milestone — if it doesn't reach $12,000–14,000/month, the cost structure won't work at any realistic revenue level.
🔴
Glendale
New Flagship — 6 Months In, Costs Look High But Context Matters
Glendale opened in September 2025 as a straight-to-flagship location and has only 6 months of operating history. Its $59,353 in service revenue over that period works out to $9,892/month — annualizing to approximately $118,700/year, which is a reasonable start for a new flagship. Rent of $27,512 over 6 months ($4,585/month) is higher than most locations, but that's expected for a larger flagship space. As a percentage of the early-stage revenue (46%), it looks alarming — but that's a function of being 6 months old, not a structural problem. For reference, Edgewater's mature rent is $2,578/month; Glendale at $4,585/month suggests a larger space that should support higher revenue capacity as it matures. The commission rate of 24.6% is actually the best in the portfolio. Watch the monthly revenue trend closely — it needs to reach $15,000+ per month to make the rent structure work long term.
!
Cherry Creek
Decent Revenue, Near-Zero Profit — Three Compounding Problems
Cherry Creek generates $216k in service revenue (healthy), but its estimated NOI margin is only 4.4% — the lowest of any profitable location. Three problems stack: (1) the highest commission rate in the portfolio at 29.3% (vs. 24–26% at most locations), (2) the highest royalty rate at 7.4%, and (3) rent consuming 11.6% of revenue with a $25,107 annual payment. Any one of these is manageable. Together, they leave almost no margin. The commission structure in particular needs review — if Cherry Creek techs are on a higher commission tier, renegotiating that structure down to portfolio norms (25%) would add approximately $9,000 in annual NOI at current revenue levels.
!
Academy
$13,707 in Interest Expense — 10x Higher Than Any Other Location
Academy carries $13,707 in interest expense for the 14-month period. The next highest location is Edgewater at $7,937. Every other location runs $1,047–$1,162. Academy's interest burden is roughly 12x the median. This strongly suggests Academy has a location-specific debt obligation — likely a build-out loan or SBA note — that doesn't exist at other locations. At 2.6% of revenue, it's not catastrophic, but it's reducing an otherwise excellent margin profile. Confirm the loan balance, rate, and payoff timeline. Once paid off, Academy's NOI jumps materially.
!
Lone Tree
Opened Feb 2025 — Decent Fundamentals Held Back by Ad Spend
Lone Tree opened in February 2025 and is 13 months in. Its $115,084 in service revenue ($8,853/month, ~$106k annualized) is modest but the cost structure is actually one of the better ones in the portfolio. Rent of only $8,186 over 13 months ($630/month, 7.1% of revenue) is excellent — comparable to Academy's legendary rent structure. The drag is advertising: $19,433 over 13 months ($1,495/month, 16.9% of revenue) at an efficiency of only $5.90 per ad dollar. The ad spend isn't generating proportionate revenue. Either the market is slower to convert, the targeting needs work, or spend should be pulled back until the organic client base builds. The underlying rent economics are genuinely good here — this location has real potential if marketing efficiency improves.
!
Parker · Powers
Essentially Zero Revenue — Status Unknown
Parker shows no service income and Powers shows only $1,042 in service revenue for the full 14-month period. Fort Collins opened in November 2025 and has only 4 months of operating history — it does not appear to have its own column in this P&L yet, which means its results may be captured elsewhere or will appear in future periods. Clarify the status of Parker and Powers specifically — if pre-opening, ensure ramp projections account for the typical 12–18 month build period seen at Littleton and Lone Tree.
05Anomalies & Items Requiring Explanation
⚠
Glendale — Royalty
Glendale Pays No Royalty Despite Having Revenue — May Not Have Reached Threshold Yet
Glendale generated $59,353 in service revenue over its first 6 months but shows $0 in royalty payments. This is likely explained by the royalty structure: many franchise agreements have a ramp-up period or minimum revenue threshold before royalties kick in. At $9,892/month, Glendale may still be below that threshold. Confirm the royalty trigger point with Hello Sugar corporate to understand when royalties will begin and model that into forward projections. Similarly, Littleton's effective royalty rate of 4.5% (vs. 5.8–6.2% at mature locations) may also reflect a partial-year threshold effect from its February 2025 opening.
⚠
Cherry Creek — Royalty Rate
7.4% Royalty Rate vs. 5.8–6.2% at All Other Locations
Cherry Creek pays $16,026 in royalties on $216,893 in service revenue — a 7.4% effective rate. Every other active location runs 5.8–6.2%. This is either a data entry error, a tiered royalty structure triggered by something specific to Cherry Creek, or a contractual anomaly. At Cherry Creek's revenue, the difference between 7.4% and 6.0% costs approximately $3,037/year. Small but worth correcting if it's an error.
⚠
Edgewater — Medical Director Fees ($4,100)
Only Location With This Expense — Suggests GFE or Laser Services
Edgewater is the only location with a Medical Director Fees line ($4,100). This is consistent with locations offering GFE-required aesthetic services (laser hair removal, etc.). If this is the case, Edgewater is operating a service line that generates different margin profiles and compliance requirements vs. waxing/sugaring. Confirm what services require the medical director, what the associated revenue is, and whether this fee structure is correctly classified.
⚠
Shared Cost Allocations
Many Expense Lines Appear Identically or Near-Identically Across All Locations
Multiple expense lines — Bank Charges ($387 at nearly every location), Corporate Events ($39 each), Merchant Service Fees ($1,271), Accounting ($977), Boulevard Software ($4,051), QuickBooks ($684) — appear at virtually identical amounts across all locations. This suggests a centralized cost allocation methodology rather than actual location-level spend. While this is a legitimate accounting approach, it means the per-location NOI figures reflect allocated overhead, not necessarily the true incremental economics of each location. Keep this in mind when comparing locations — a location's true unit economics may look better or worse than the allocated P&L suggests.
⚠
Not Specified Column — $294,912 Depreciation + $4,415 Amortization
Large Non-Cash Charges Not Allocated to Locations
The "Not Specified" column contains $294,912 in depreciation and $4,415 in amortization — totaling nearly $300k in non-cash charges that are not allocated to any individual location. This is significant: if added back, the portfolio's true cash-basis NOI is materially higher than the accounting NOI. These figures should be included in any EBITDA calculation for financing or valuation purposes. Additionally, a -$10,500 Owner Salary entry appears in this column, which is unusual and may represent an adjustment or credit.

07Location Verdicts
Edgewater
Portfolio Star
The crown jewel. Best revenue, best ad efficiency, excellent margins. The Medical Director expense is the one item to understand and document. Everything else is firing on all cylinders. Double down on what's working here.
Academy
Portfolio Star
A textbook mature Hello Sugar location. Exceptional rent leverage, controlled commissions, solid ad returns. The only cloud is $13,707 in interest expense that's suppressing what should be a 30%+ NOI margin. Identify and plan the payoff of that debt obligation — the upside once it's gone is significant.
Aurora
Solid Performer
Reliable 15% NOI with room to grow. Rent at 12.9% is the main drag — if the lease comes up, negotiate hard. Commission rates are among the best in the portfolio. A steady, mature location.
Westminster
Solid Performer
Nearly identical profile to Aurora — similar revenue, similar margins, similar ad efficiency. Growing and stable. The opportunity here is modest improvements on multiple fronts rather than any single dramatic fix.
Boulder
Underrated
Lowest rent in the portfolio outside Academy at 6.4% — a structural advantage that shows up in the 18.3% estimated NOI margin despite lower absolute revenue. Revenue growth here is highly efficient due to the fixed cost base. Invest in growing this one.
Cherry Creek
Fix Commission Structure
Revenue isn't the problem — it's everything downstream. Commission rate needs to come down from 29.3% to portfolio norms. Royalty rate anomaly needs investigation. Without fixing the commission structure, this location will never produce meaningful profit regardless of revenue growth.
Lone Tree
Fix Ad Efficiency
Open 13 months with excellent rent economics ($630/month) that give this location real long-term potential. The drag is ad efficiency — $5.90 per ad dollar is not working at current revenue levels. Pull back or redirect ad spend, lean into organic and membership conversion instead. The bones are good here.
Littleton
Revenue Growth Required
Open 13 months at $8,544/month — not where a year-old location should be. The rent ($1,471/mo) is manageable if revenue reaches $12k+/month; the ad spend ($1,499/mo) needs to start pulling its weight. This is not an emergency exit situation, but it needs a focused 90-day push on client acquisition and retention before it becomes one.
Glendale
Early Stage — Watch Monthly Trend
Only 6 months old and opened straight to flagship. The high rent and ad percentages are entirely expected at this stage — every new location looks like this in months 1–6. Commission discipline at 24.6% is the best in the portfolio, which is a great sign. The question to answer: is monthly revenue trending up month-over-month? If Glendale is on a consistent growth curve, no action needed. If revenue has plateaued below $12k/month, start addressing the ramp strategy now while the location is still early enough to course-correct.
